Principle 40: Composite Materials
Replace a homogeneous material with a composite that combines components whose joint properties none of them has alone. Examples: carbon-fiber-reinforced plastic aircraft wings that are stronger than aluminum yet far lighter and corrosion-resistant; fiberglass boats and surfboards lighter and easier to shape than wood; Tetra Pak cartons layering cardboard for stiffness, polyethylene for sealing and aluminum as a light barrier.
