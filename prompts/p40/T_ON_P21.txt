Use TRIZ to solve the following engineering problem.

In your solution, apply TRIZ Inventive Principle 21: Skipping.
Perform harmful, hazardous or delicate stages at very high speed, so fast that damage has no time to develop. Examples: thin-walled plastic tubes cut at very high speed so inertia keeps them from deforming; a finger moved quickly through a flame without being burned; a flip book whose rapidly turned pages merge into fluid motion.
Base your solution on this principle, adapting it to the specifics of the problem.
