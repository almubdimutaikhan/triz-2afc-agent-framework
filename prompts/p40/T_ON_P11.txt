In your solution, apply TRIZ Inventive Principle 11: In-advance Cushioning.
Compensate for the limited reliability of a system by preparing emergency countermeasures beforehand, so that when failure or overload occurs the damage is absorbed. Examples: a pressure relief valve that vents before a tank can burst; a car airbag that inflates into a gas cushion before the head strikes the steering wheel; a protective phone case that absorbs drops and impacts.
Base your solution on this principle, adapting it to the specifics of the problem.
