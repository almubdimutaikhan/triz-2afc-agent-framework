In your solution, apply TRIZ Inventive Principle 34: Discarding and Recovering.
Discard, dissolve or evaporate parts of the system once they have fulfilled their function, or restore consumable parts directly during operation. Examples: spent cartridge cases ejected to make room for the next round; booster rockets dropped at altitude and recovered for reuse; snap-off blades broken away to expose a fresh edge; rechargeable batteries regenerated by recharging.
Base your solution on this principle, adapting it to the specifics of the problem.
